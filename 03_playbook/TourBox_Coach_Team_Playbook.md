# 1. Why This Document Exists

We're starting something new at TourBox. After 7 years of serving creative professionals, we're expanding into a much larger market: **300 million aspiring artists who want to draw but don't know where to start.**

This isn't a side project. It's a strategic bet on the next decade of TourBox. It will involve software, hardware, AI, curriculum, education partnerships, and entirely new go-to-market channels.

That means **alignment matters more than ever**. When you have 30+ people working across software, AI, hardware, and content, small misalignments compound into wasted quarters. This document is the antidote.

**Three things this doc is for:**

1. **Onboarding** — New team members read this first. By the end, they understand what we're building, why, and how their role fits.
2. **Decision-making** — When you face a fork in the road, this doc tells you what we'd usually do and why. If your case is an exception, document it.
3. **Re-alignment** — When a team starts drifting (it happens), this doc is the gravity that pulls us back.

**Three things this doc is NOT:**

1. **Not a project plan.** Project plans live in our PM tool. This is the strategy and the principles, not the Gantt chart.
2. **Not immutable.** Reality changes. When we learn something new, we update this. Markup history matters.
3. **Not a substitute for talking.** When you're confused, ask your lead. The doc is a starting point, not the final word.

# 2. Where We're Going

## Vision (3-year horizon)

> **TourBox becomes the platform that teaches the world to draw.**

Within three years, "TourBox" should mean something different to a 14-year-old aspiring manga artist than it does to a 35-year-old retoucher today. Same company, same values, dramatically expanded reach.

## Mission (what we do every day)

> We give beginners the tactile tools, AI guidance, and structured curriculum they need to become artists — not by replacing their effort, but by scaffolding it.

The verbs that matter: **scaffold, guide, encourage, teach.** We are not a generation tool. We are a teaching tool.

## What success looks like

By end of Year 1:
- A person who has never drawn before can pick up TourBox Coach, follow lessons, and produce work they're proud of within 30 days.
- Their parents trust the device because all AI runs locally, no data leaves the box.
- That person tells their friends. We grow through delight, not paid acquisition.

By end of Year 3:
- TourBox Coach is in art classrooms, gift guides, and YouTube tutorials worldwide.
- We are the default answer to "I want to learn to draw — what should I get?"
- Our marketplace lets working artists earn meaningful income teaching their craft.

## What it does NOT look like

- **Not** another AI image generator. Midjourney, DALL·E, and Flux already serve that market.
- **Not** a Wacom replacement for pros (though we still serve pros — see Pro Track).
- **Not** a closed walled garden. Privacy and openness matter.
- **Not** a subscription-first business. Hardware is primary; subscription is optional.

# 3. The Strategic Bet

We are betting that **three converging trends create a once-per-decade window:**

## Bet #1: Edge AI just became cheap enough

Mobile-class chips (Snapdragon 8 Gen 3, Dimensity 9300) now run useful vision and small diffusion models at 30+ TOPS for under $80 BOM cost, fanless. Two years ago, this hardware would have needed a fan and cost $400. Two years from now, every consumer device will have this. **The window is 12–18 months.**

## Bet #2: AI tutoring is an unsolved problem

Generative AI is commoditized. Tutoring AI — the kind that watches your work, gives helpful feedback, and adapts to your skill level — is not. Nobody has cracked it for visual arts. The reference is Duolingo: they spent a decade making language learning a daily habit. We can do the same for drawing, and the technology to do it now exists for the first time.

## Bet #3: Hardware is our defensible advantage

Software is being commoditized by AI. Hardware with deep IP, manufacturing relationships, and tactile UX is much harder to replicate. TourBox has 7 years of haptic IP, ergonomic patents, and brand trust in the creative community. **This is our moat. We must use it.**

## What we're NOT betting on

- We are **not** betting on Apple or Adobe staying out forever. They will enter eventually. Our 12-month head start is everything.
- We are **not** betting on cloud AI. Privacy matters too much in our target segment (kids, education).
- We are **not** betting on a hit single product. We're building a multi-year category.

## How we know if we're right

We have explicit gate metrics at the end of Year 0 (see Section 9). If we miss them, we kill or pivot. No ego. No sunk-cost.

# 5. Year 0 Deep Dive

This is where most of us are working right now. The most detailed section of this playbook.

## Phase 0a (Months 1–3): Build the MVP

**Mission:** Get a usable, paid AI Tutor app into the hands of 100 alpha users by end of Month 3.

**Weeks 5–6: AI feedback loop**

**Weeks 7–9: Lesson library**
- 20 starter lessons fully scripted, illustrated, and tested
- Onboarding flow with skill assessment (Day 1 experience)
- Progress dashboard
- Parent-friendly mode (kid usage analytics)

**Weeks 10–12: Polish + alpha launch**
- Bug fix and polish
- Launch alpha to 100 users from existing customer base
- Set up feedback collection
- Prepare beta launch playbook

| # | Deliverable | Owner | Done When |

## Phase 0b (Months 4–6): Beta and iterate

**Mission:** Reach 1,000 paid beta users and validate the four gate metrics.

### Activities

**Beta launch (Month 4)**
- Open beta to existing TourBox customers + waitlist (target: 500 in first 2 weeks)
- Pricing test: $19/mo, $39/quarter, $99/year — see what converts
- Active community channel (Discord) for beta users
- Weekly beta updates with new content

**Iteration loop (Months 4–6)**
- Weekly cohort analysis: who comes back, who churns, why
- Lesson-level analytics: which lessons frustrate, which delight
- Curriculum adjustments based on data
- Feature requests prioritized by retention impact

**Hardware spec validation (Month 5)**
- Profile AI inference cost across device types
- Identify which Year 1 hardware specs match real usage
- Make/break decisions on AI Companion Box BOM

**Gate review prep (Month 6)**
- Compile data for executive review
- Year 1 budget request
- Hardware go/no-go decision

### Gate metrics (must hit ALL four)

| Metric | Target | Why it matters |
|--------|--------|----------------|
| Paid beta users | 1,000+ | Proves people will pay, not just try |
| 30-day retention | 40%+ | Proves the product is sticky |
| NPS from beginner segment | 40+ | Proves we're solving the right problem |
| Willingness to pay (monthly) | $15+ | Proves unit economics work |

If we miss any one: we don't proceed to Year 1 hardware. We pivot or kill.

# 7. Workstreams in Detail

## 7.1 Curriculum Workstream

**Owner:** Curriculum Lead

**North star:** Build the pedagogical framework that takes someone from "I've never drawn" to "I can finish a piece I'm proud of" in 30–90 days of regular use.

**Year 0 deliverables:**
- 20 starter lessons (Phase 0a) → 100+ by Year 1 launch
- Pedagogical framework document (how we think about teaching)
- Lesson template + production pipeline
- Skill assessment system (Day 1)
- Progress tracking schema

**Lesson categories (Year 0 starter set):**
1. Foundational marks (5 lessons) — line confidence, basic shapes
2. Observation (4 lessons) — proportion, basic perspective
3. Construction (4 lessons) — building forms from primitives
4. Faces (4 lessons) — proportions, emotion, simple portraits
5. Style transfer (3 lessons) — applying lessons to anime, cartoon, realistic

**Working principles:**
- Every lesson must be completable in 15–30 minutes
- Every lesson must have a "win" — something to be proud of at the end
- No lesson assumes prior art knowledge
- Progressive difficulty curve: 80% of users complete each next lesson
- Voice is encouraging but specific (avoid "nice job!" — say "nice control on that long line")

**Tools:**
- Lesson authoring tool (build internally, simple JSON + asset bundle)
- Notion-based content review pipeline
- Internal testing rotation: every lesson reviewed by 3 people before alpha

## 7.2 Software Engineering Workstream

**Owner:** Engineering Lead

**North star:** Ship a delightful, fast, reliable cross-platform app that beginners love using daily.

**Year 0 architecture decisions (must be made by Week 2):**

| Decision | Options | Recommendation |
|----------|---------|----------------|
| Mobile codebase | Native (Swift + Kotlin) / React Native / Flutter | Native — drawing performance and TourBox SDK matter |
| Desktop framework | Electron / Tauri / Native | Tauri — smaller, faster, better feeling |
| State management | Standard per platform | TBD by tech lead |
| Backend | Firebase / Custom / Supabase | Supabase initially — fast, can migrate later |
| Analytics | PostHog / Amplitude / Mixpanel | PostHog — open source, can self-host for privacy |

**Engineering principles:**
- **Performance is a feature.** A laggy drawing experience kills retention. Test on 3-year-old hardware.
- **Privacy is non-negotiable.** Default to no data collection. Explicit opt-in for everything else.
- **Simplicity over cleverness.** A 14-year-old should be able to use the app, so the code should be simple enough that a new engineer is productive in week 1.
- **Test before merging.** No exceptions for "small changes."
- **Accessibility from day one.** Color contrast, font sizing, screen reader support.

**Code quality bar:**
- All code reviewed by another engineer before merge
- 80% test coverage minimum on business logic (drawing engine, lesson player)
- Performance budget: app startup <2s, lesson load <500ms, stroke latency <16ms
- Crash rate target: <0.1% sessions

## 7.3 AI/ML Workstream

**Owner:** AI Lead

**North star:** AI feedback that beginners trust — encouraging, specific, kind, useful, and on-device by Year 1.

**Year 0 model strategy:**

| Capability | Year 0 (cloud-first) | Year 1 (on-device) |
|------------|---------------------|--------------------|
| Stroke analysis | GPT-4V / Claude (via API) | Custom small VLM (~3B params) |
| Proportion checking | MediaPipe + heuristics | Same, optimized |
| Lesson recommendation | Server-side rules | On-device personalization |
| Tone of feedback | RLHF-tuned LLM | Distilled smaller LLM |

**Why cloud-first in Year 0:**
- Faster iteration on quality
- We need data to train Year 1 on-device models
- Beta users explicitly opt in to cloud during beta phase

**Why on-device by Year 1:**
- Privacy promise to parents and educators
- Latency: feedback must feel instant
- Cost: cloud inference at scale eats margin

**Feedback quality principles:**
1. **Specific over generic.** "Your line is wobbly here" beats "good job."
2. **One thing at a time.** Beginners can't process 5 critiques.
3. **Encouraging frame.** Lead with what works, then suggest improvement.
4. **No shame.** Never use words like "wrong," "bad," "incorrect."
5. **Age-aware.** Tone for 14-year-old ≠ tone for 40-year-old.

**Safety reviews:**
- All AI output reviewed for tone, safety, and appropriateness
- Specific safeguards for kid users (no critical or harsh language)
- Quarterly red-team for adversarial inputs

# 8. Working Principles & Culture

## How we make decisions

**1. Default to action.** A wrong decision made fast is usually better than a correct decision made slow. We can correct course quickly.

**2. Disagree and commit.** When the team is split, the Accountable person makes the call. Everyone else commits and stops re-litigating.

**3. Strong opinions, loosely held.** Have a perspective. Argue for it. Update when you see new evidence.

**4. Killing is success.** When something isn't working, killing it is a win, not a failure. Sunk cost is the enemy.

**5. Customer over hierarchy.** If the user research says one thing and the leader says another, the leader doesn't automatically win. Bring the data.

## How we work

**Speed over polish in Year 0.** We're validating, not perfecting. Ship the rough version, learn, then polish what matters.

**Async first.** Default to writing. Save synchronous time for unblocking, brainstorming, and connection.

**Documentation is the product.** If it's not written down, it doesn't exist. Decisions, designs, and learnings all get docs.

**Small batches.** Break work into 1–2 week chunks. Long projects without milestones drift.

**No heroes.** Sustainable pace beats sprints to burnout. We have years of work ahead.

## How we treat each other

- **Be kind, be direct.** Both. Niceness without honesty is poison; bluntness without kindness is poison too.
- **Assume good intent.** When something seems off, ask first, accuse never.
- **Credit generously.** Recognize specific work by specific people.
- **Disagree with ideas, not people.** "I think this approach is wrong because X" not "you are wrong."
- **Make space for newcomers.** Loudest voice ≠ best idea. Pull quiet people into the conversation.

# 10. Risk Register

We track risks explicitly. Each has an owner. Each has a watch metric. Quarterly review and update.

## Top risks

### R1: Beginner segment doesn't validate

**Probability:** Medium  
**Impact:** Critical (kills the program)  
**Owner:** Product Manager  
**Watch metric:** Year 0 gate metrics — especially 30-day retention  
**Mitigation:** Phase 0a structured to fail fast. By Month 4 we know if retention is heading toward 40%. If trending below 25% by Month 4, we pivot before spending Q3 budget.

### R2: AI feedback quality is too generic

**Probability:** Medium-High  
**Impact:** High (kills retention even if everything else works)  
**Owner:** AI Lead  
**Watch metric:** "Feedback felt helpful" rate during alpha and beta  
**Mitigation:** Co-design with art teachers from day one. Manual review of all alpha feedback samples. RLHF tuning monthly.

### R3: Apple or Adobe enters this space

**Probability:** Medium (12-month horizon)  
**Impact:** High  
**Owner:** Initiative Lead  
**Watch metric:** Industry news monitoring, patent filings  
**Mitigation:** Curriculum and community moats compound over time. Move fast in Year 0–1. By Year 2 we should be the obvious answer in the category.

### R4: Hardware delays push timeline

**Probability:** Medium  
**Impact:** Medium-High  
**Owner:** Hardware Lead (when hired)  
**Watch metric:** Sample availability, certification timelines  
**Mitigation:** Off-the-shelf reference designs only. No custom silicon in Year 1. Maintain second-source supplier relationships.

### R5: TourBox brand is wrong for beginner segment

**Probability:** Low-Medium  
**Impact:** Medium  
**Owner:** Marketing Lead  
**Watch metric:** Beginner brand survey scores  
**Mitigation:** Sub-brand "Coach" positioning. Different distribution channels. Testing with non-existing-customers in beta.

### R6: Privacy / safety incident with kid user

**Probability:** Low  
**Impact:** Critical (brand-defining)  
**Owner:** Legal/Security + AI Lead  
**Watch metric:** All AI outputs sampled and reviewed  
**Mitigation:** On-device processing by Year 1. Manual safety review of feedback model outputs. Clear parental controls. Bug bounty program.

### R7: Team burnout

**Probability:** Medium  
**Impact:** High  
**Owner:** Initiative Lead + each manager  
**Watch metric:** Team pulse survey (monthly), unplanned turnover  
**Mitigation:** Realistic timelines. No heroics culture. 4-day on-call rotations. Mandatory time off after major launches.

## Quarterly pre-mortem

Every quarter, in a 60-minute meeting, we ask: *"It's 6 months from now. The program failed. What killed it?"*

Each lead writes their answer privately. We share, cluster, and update the risk register. This catches new risks the project plan misses.

# 12. Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is the pro market dead to us now?**  
A: No. We have a parallel pro track (Section 4) protecting and expanding for our 300K existing customers. Beginner is the new bet, not the only bet.

**Q: What if we miss the Year 0 gate metrics?**  
A: We pivot or kill. We've explicitly designed Year 0 to fail fast and cheaply. Better to spend $1.5M and learn than $10M and learn the same thing later.

**Q: How is this different from Procreate / Krita / Adobe Fresco?**  
A: They're tools. We're a teacher. They assume you know what you're doing; we assume you don't and walk you through it.

**Q: Why on-device AI? Cloud is easier.**  
A: Two reasons. (1) Privacy — our target users include kids and education. Cloud AI is a non-starter. (2) Cost at scale — cloud inference at 100K monthly active users would eat our margin.

**Q: Why not just make a software-only product?**  
A: Hardware is our defensible advantage. Software-only puts us in race-to-the-bottom land with infinite competitors. Hardware + curriculum + AI is a moat.

**Q: What if Apple / Adobe / Google launches this first?**  
A: They might. Our 12-month head start matters. Curriculum and community moats compound over time. We've planned for this — see Risk R3.

**Q: Will we kill the existing TourBox products?**  
A: No. They continue. Same factory, same supply chain, same team for those. Coach is a new line, not a replacement.

**Q: Why is this called "TourBox Coach" and not something else?**  
A: Working name. Could change. Marketing Lead owns final naming.

**Q: What's the relationship to Anthropic / OpenAI / etc.?**  
A: We use cloud LLMs (Claude, GPT-4V) in Year 0 for speed. We're moving to on-device by Year 1. We don't build foundation models.

**Q: Can I work on both Coach and the pro track?**  
A: Generally no — context switching kills productivity. Some shared infrastructure roles are exceptions (e.g., TourBox SDK engineering).

**Q: What if I disagree with the strategy?**  
A: Talk to your lead. If still unresolved, talk to the Initiative Lead. Disagreement is healthy; silent resentment is not.

**Q: When do I see the new TourBox prototype?**  
A: Year 1 prototype shows late Q4 of Year 0. Until then, software is on the existing TourBox.
