1 · Executive Summary

Every individual capability already ships in production-grade form somewhere in industry. The novelty is the combination: a real-time collaborative canvas, Copilot-style stroke autocompletion, and a Duolingo-style gamified curriculum, wrapped around an AI companion that draws with the user.

QuestionShort Answer
Drawly-like real-time, multi-user drawing canvas?Yes. tldraw sync, Yjs, Liveblocks already do this at scale.
AI drawing with you stroke-by-stroke in real time?Yes, latency is the engineering challenge. Sketch-RNN baseline; 2025–2026 models (StrokeFusion, SwiftSketch, VideoSketcher) raise the ceiling.
AutoDraw's "complete my doodle"?Yes — QuickDraw classifier + curated clip-art library.
Copilot-style ghosted next-stroke (Tab to accept)?Yes. Sketch-RNN's original 2017 demo literally does this.
AI chat / voice companion during drawing?Yes. OpenAI Realtime API (GPT-4o-realtime), Gemini Live, ElevenLabs.
Gamified daily lessons (ArtWorkout / Duolingo)?Yes. Pure product engineering. Stroke-accuracy via DTW + Fréchet.
Hardest engineering riskEnd-to-end ghost-stroke latency < 150 ms on mid-tier hardware.
Hardest product riskAvoiding the "Clippy problem" — suggestions that feel patronizing.

2 · Reference App Analysis

Drawly Quality bar

getdrawly.com · drawly.app

What works: stroke-level liveness, instant-room URLs, zero-install web entry, brutally simple toolset.

Stack signals: Firebase Realtime DB for sync — fine, but cost-inefficient at scale and CRDT-less.

Copy: presence cursors URL-to-room < 2 s

Skip: no AI assist anywhere — that's literally our gap.

Google AutoDraw Min AI bar

autodraw.com

How it works: the QuickDraw RNN classifier trained on 1B+ doodles; the UI offers professionally-drawn clip-art replacements.

Limits: only replaces, never autocompletes mid-drawing; no teaching, no companion.

Reuse: the classifier as a category prior to bias our generative stroke model.

ArtWorkout Curriculum bar

artworkout.app

How it works: 2,500+ bite-sized lessons; stroke accuracy + pressure scored several times per second; daily energy, streaks, multiplayer mode, Loomis-method & anatomy courses.

Reuse: trace-overlay → real-time score → retry-to-improve loop; the daily-streak/energy economy.

Improve: replace pure-geometry feedback with an LLM tutor that explains the why (proportion, weight, perspective).

4 · Scope of Work

Phase 0 — Spike & Validate

~2 weeks

tldraw + Yjs canvas; Sketch-RNN in browser via TF.js / ONNX-Web; measure first-stroke latency on mid-tier hardware. Decide edge vs. server inference.

Phase 1 — MVP "DrawCopilot v0.1"

~8–10 weeks · single-user

Vector canvas, stroke-event stream, F3 (AutoDraw clone), F4 (ghost-stroke + Tab-to-accept), accounts, project save/load, telemetry from day 1 (this becomes our training flywheel).

Phase 2 — "Draw With Me" social layer

~6–8 weeks

Drawly-parity multiplayer rooms (tldraw sync or Yjs + y-websocket on Cloudflare Durable Objects). AI companion v1 joins as a participant. Optional voice via OpenAI Realtime API.

Phase 3 — "Learn With Me" curriculum

~6–8 weeks

Lesson authoring tool, real-time scoring (DTW + per-stroke metrics), AI tutor (GPT-4o-vision rates & narrates corrections via Realtime API). Streaks, energy, XP, league boards.

Phase 4 — Polish, mobile, growth

ongoing

iPad PWA + Capacitor wrapper for Pencil pressure & palm rejection. Custom-trained stroke models on our own data. Gallery / community. Image-to-lesson pipeline.

Latency budget — ghost-stroke suggestions

PointerUp → stroke event ready
10 ms
Stroke encode + inference (browser, WebGPU)
80 ms
Render ghost overlay
20 ms
User-perceived total
≤ 150 ms

Anything above ~250 ms feels "AI is thinking" instead of "AI is drawing with me." This is the single most important non-functional requirement.

7 · Risks & Mitigations

RiskSeverityMitigation
Ghost-stroke latency feels sluggish on mid-range devicesHighShip a small (≤5 MB) on-device model first; server inference as fallback; cache recent latent states.
AI suggestions feel annoying (the "Clippy problem")HighOpt-in (Tab to accept); ghosts auto-fade; user-tunable intensity 0–100%.
Copyright / training-data concernsMediumTrain on QuickDraw (CC-licensed) only; commission original artist demos for higher-quality fine-tunes; clear opt-in for user-data training.
Realtime API costs at scale (voice)MediumVoice opt-in, per-session, capped duration; downgrade to text when idle.
Multiplayer abuse (NSFW, harassment)MediumVision moderation (Azure Content Safety / OpenAI Moderation) on canvas snapshots; report/mute; share-link gated rooms.
Mobile Safari quirks with WebGPUMediumDetect & fall back to WebGL; further fall back to server inference.
Curriculum quality at scaleMediumStart with 30 expert-authored lessons; only invest in auto-lesson pipeline after retention is proven.
Differentiation vs. Procreate / Drawly / ArtWorkoutStrategicWedge = the combination + the Copilot UX. None of the incumbents have ghosted next-stroke suggestions.

8 · Brainstorm — Ideas We're Sitting On

  1. Persona marketplace. Let creators publish AI companions ("Bob Ross", "Studio Ghibli senpai", "grumpy art prof"). Persona = system prompt + voice + drawing-style fine-tune. Revenue share.
  2. Replay & timelapse. Every stroke is a typed event → free time-lapse export. Great for TikTok / IG growth.
  3. "Tab-to-improve" globally. Beyond next-stroke: Tab on a finished drawing → AI proposes a whole-drawing polish that you accept stroke-by-stroke.
  4. Daily challenge multiplayer. ArtWorkout-style daily prompt, drawn live with a stranger + an AI judge. Combines all three pillars in one feature.
  5. Drawing-style transfer. Trained on the user's own past drawings, the companion adapts to their style.
  6. Accessibility angle. Ghost-stroke + voice tutor is unusually friendly for users with motor-control or visual-processing differences. Worth designing for explicitly.
  7. Education partnerships. ArtWorkout proves the consumer market; the real TAM expansion is K-12 and adult-ed bundles.
  8. Local-first option. "Private mode": Sketch-RNN runs entirely in-browser, no server hop, no telemetry. Privacy is a moat against bigger players.

9 · Open Questions for the Team

  1. Brand positioning: "Copilot for drawing" (developer-flavored) vs. "Your AI drawing buddy" (consumer-flavored)? Pick before any UI work — they imply different aesthetics.
  2. Monetization: subscription (ArtWorkout-style) vs. credits (per-AI-call) vs. freemium with cosmetic brushes?
  3. Default companion behavior: draws unprompted in shared rooms, or only when summoned? Strong opinion: only when summoned, in v1.
  4. Platform priority: Web-first (largest reach, easiest dev) vs. iPad-first (best pen UX, highest WTP)? Suggest web-first, iPad-PWA fast-follow.
  5. Data strategy: opt-in donate-your-strokes flywheel? With what rev-share or perks?

10 · Recommendation

Proceed. Build the Phase-0 spike in two weeks. The decisive question is not whether the technology exists — it does — but whether we can keep ghost-stroke latency under ~150 ms on a mid-tier laptop. If we hit that bar in the spike, every other feature is "just engineering" and well-precedented.

The strategic insight: none of Drawly, AutoDraw, or ArtWorkout currently ships a Copilot-style stroke-completion UX. That is our wedge. The collaborative canvas and the gamified curriculum are table stakes we add to make the wedge a product.
